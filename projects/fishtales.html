Fish Tales

Reimagining tinned fish through accessible ethics and design-forward packaging.

Brand & Packaging Designer · Team of 2 · 5 weeks · 2025

Overview

Role

Brand & packaging designer

Team

2 designers

Duration

5 weeks (2025)

Tools used

Figma, Illustrator, InDesign, Photoshop

Role: Led packaging concept, fishing method illustrations, tin design, and digital applications. Co-led research and brand strategy.

The Opportunity

The Australian tinned fish market is polarized:

  • Supermarket brands: Affordable, generic, vague sustainability
  • Specialty brands: Beautiful, transparent, expensive

Gap: No accessible brand combining affordability, transparency, and design appeal.

Research Insights

Focus group (7 participants, 19–55):

  • 85% rated sustainability extremely important
  • 71% preferred modern/minimal packaging
  • 100% would read sourcing info on pack
  • Willing to pay 20–30% more, not 300% more

Core insight: Transparency must be embedded into the packaging itself.

Brand Strategy

Position: Anchored in Authenticity.

Target: 20–40 year olds, ethically minded, design conscious, urban.

Price point: $3.49 per tin — accessible but elevated.

Color System

Early iterations: Dark, muted, overly exclusive.

Final system: Primary muted blues for trust. Secondary bright accents per product: Tuna → Sea Grass; Oysters → Sailor's Warning; Sardines → Sailor's Delight. Balances credibility and approachability.

Book-Inspired Packaging Format

Portrait orientation inspired by book structure: front cover, spine, back story. Creates shelf differentiation and narrative pacing.

Fishing Method Illustrations

Hand-sketched then vectorized illustrations showing: Pole & Line (Tuna), Purse Seine without FADs (Sardines), Oyster Farming (Oysters). Makes sustainability specific and visible.

Material as Message

Printed on 100% recycled kraft boxboard. Background left unprinted. Texture communicates sustainability visibly.

Final System

Includes: box designs (3 products), tin tops, logo variations, website prototype, social media grid, POS materials. Modular color system enables extension.

Reflection

  • Specificity builds trust.
  • Constraints can become defining assets.
  • Premium and accessible require balance.
  • Transparency must be visible, not implied.
