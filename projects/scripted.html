AI-Supported Conversation Practice for Intermediate Language Learners

UX/UI Designer

4 designers

10 weeks (2025)

Tools

My role

  • Led recording experience design and navigation architecture
  • Independently redesigned achievement system after usability testing

Project Domain

Problem domain

Intermediate language learners often plateau. They understand grammar but struggle with real-time conversational fluency.

Existing tools:

  • Lesson apps — structured but not conversational
  • Community apps — conversational but lack structured feedback
  • Tutors — effective but expensive and scheduled

Design challenge

How might we provide structured feedback on real conversations without disrupting conversational flow?

Process Timeline

Phase 1 — Concept development (group)

  • Developed 4 distinct concepts
  • Created mid-fidelity wireframes
  • Conducted concept validation with 12 participants
  • Recording concept ranked highest for usefulness

Key insight: Users valued feedback but lacked consistent practice partners.

Phase 2 — Hybrid solution (group)

We combined recording-based feedback with AI + solo conversation access. This removed dependency on external partners and expanded accessibility.

Phase 3 — High-fidelity prototype (group)

Built complete mobile prototype including: Home, Recording (3 modes), Transcript review, Personalized lessons, Progress tracking.

My Core Contribution

Recording experience design

I led the design of the recording interaction and navigation system.

Design goals:

  • Minimize cognitive load during live conversation
  • Avoid screen dependency
  • Provide optional support without interruption

Design decisions:

Before: Clear entry — Solo | Group | AI.

During: Minimal UI (waveform, timer); optional live transcript; subtle word support popup.

After: Automatic summary-first feedback.

Usability Testing

Tested with 8 intermediate language learners. Method: Think-aloud protocol, 4 structured tasks, pre & post questionnaires.

Key findings

  • Progress visualization motivates users
  • Support must be unobtrusive
  • Transcript review is overwhelming
  • Visual design lacks personality

Ease of use: 4.1/5. 100% task completion for recording flow.

Iteration

Problem: Transcript review caused cognitive overload.

Iteration: Summary-first design; categorized mistake types; weakness pattern tracking; full transcript secondary.

Result: Improved clarity and scannability.

My Independent Extension

Achievement system redesign

After final testing, I independently analyzed engagement gaps and redesigned the motivational layer.

Problem: Users did not feel rewarded when reviewing transcripts.

Solution: Shift from error-based feedback to progress-based reinforcement.

Features: Achievement cards; conversation milestones; goal setting; progress framing. This redesign focused on emotional reinforcement and habit formation.

Experience Journey

Complete user flow: Start Conversation → Record → AI Analysis → Summary → Lesson → Progress.

Reflection

  • Accessibility constraints shape product direction — Solo and AI modes emerged from the need to support users without consistent partners.
  • Usability does not equal engagement — The achievement redesign addressed motivation, not just clarity.
  • Summary-first feedback reduces overwhelm — Prioritizing the summary over the full transcript improved clarity and scannability.
  • Leading a core interaction flow clarified my strength in interaction design — Owning the recording experience reinforced my focus on reducing cognitive load and supporting flow.
